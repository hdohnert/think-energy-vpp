FAQs

Think Energy will be your electricity provider. Your electricity bill and service will come from us — just like any other provider in Texas. We’ll also provide and manage your backup battery system.

When there’s no outage, we use your battery to perform grid services that support Texas’s electricity system. This allows us to recover the cost of your battery and installation — while still offering you a low, fixed electricity rate. You get backup power and savings, and we handle everything behind the scenes.

Your battery duration depends on how much power you use, your battery size, and the charge level at the time of the outage. Here’s a general breakdown:

  • Low usage (750W — lights, fridge, some A/C):
    • 1 battery: 20–30 hrs
    • 2 batteries: 40–60 hrs
  • Average usage (1.5kW — typical appliances, A/C):
    • 1 battery: 10–15 hrs
    • 2 batteries: 20–30 hrs
  • High usage (8kW — electric heaters, heavy A/C):
    • 1 battery: 2–3 hrs
    • 2 batteries: 3–5 hrs

Battery charge is typically high before an outage and never starts below 20%. If you have solar or a generator, it can extend your backup time further.

Installation is usually completed in one day between 8 AM and 6 PM. Our team handles everything:

  • We’ll drop off materials in advance.
  • The crew arrives between 8–10 AM.
  • Expect a 1–3 hour power shutoff while we wire your home for backup.
  • We return to collect leftover materials the same day or next.

You don’t need to be home unless your breaker is in a locked garage or you want to guide battery placement.
